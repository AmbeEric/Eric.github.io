Brief History

The Cameroon Development Corporation (CDC) is a state-owned agro-industrial complex in Cameroon, West Africa. Here's a brief history: - 1947: The British colonial administration establishes the CDC to develop and manage agricultural plantations in the Southwest Region of Cameroon. - 1954: The CDC begins operating as a public corporation, focusing on banana, rubber, and palm oil production. - 1960: Cameroon gains independence from France, and the CDC becomes a key player in the country's economic development.

Today, the CDC is one of Cameroon's largest employers and a significant contributor to the country's economy. However, it still faces challenges related to global market competition, climate change, and local community engagement.
